Our Philosophy

We invest behind exceptional teams, at the concept stage. We love to back teams working on bold transformative technology and disruptive business models, as their first institutional investor

How we work with you?

We are active investors, working with a small number of companies every year, and committing our time to work with the founders closely. We bring in operational experience from some of the most respected companies in the world and a deep experience in the venture ecosystem, to help founders solve problems and scale fast. We connect you to our network of seasoned founders and operators in India and in the Silicon Valley, who can provide invaluable subject matter guidance.

Consumer Demand

Driven by a large young population and their rising income levels, India is emerging as one of the largest consumer markets in the world. At the same time, the supply continues to be largely fragmented and unorganised, leading to a shortfall in quality and fulfillment. We believe this is an incredible environment to create value and growth through technology and business model innovation, and provide quality products and services at mass scale and affordability. We are especially interested in marketplace models in areas like retail, fintech, healthcare, logistics etc.

About Fundraise

How is raising seed round from a fund, different from raising capital from angel investors?
A seed fund is in the business of investing in companies on full time basis, and therefore is able to build systems and processes of engagement with its portfolio companies in the long term. On the flip side, since a seed fund usually has a team making the investment decision, the timelines involved here could be slightly longer. Since an angel investor is an individual investing in their personal capacity, the initial investment decision may be reached sooner. However, they usually do not fund companies as their day - job, and hence the engagement model with angels is usually variable, depending on their other time commitments. That said, many times, angel investors bring in a strong strategic value to the company, particularly if they are from the same industry. We at vcfirm are open to partnering with such co-investors.
What is the right dilution in each of these cases?
There is no ‘right’ answer for this. Institutional money is typically always long term with ability to support in future if required. In our view, institutional money should always be prefered to an angel unless the angel brings in strategic value and is willing to give some time to the founders. A mix of fund and angel might be ideal with the institution leading.
How is the fund raise environment at the moment?
The fund raise environment for new company creation is quite optimistic. Committed founders with a long term vision are not finding it a challenge to engage in serious investor discussions. Various innovative business models are being funded, and we at vcfirm are actively looking for attractive investment opportunities.
